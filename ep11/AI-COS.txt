AI CHIEF OF STAFF
STEP 3: CORE WORKFLOWS

These workflows are where the AI CoS creates leverage.
They turn context + integrations into execution.

Run these consistently.
This is how the system compounds.

==================================================
WORKFLOW 1: DAILY COMMAND BRIEF
==================================================

When to use:
- First thing every morning

Purpose:
- Start the day with clarity
- Align time, priorities, and decisions
- Prevent reactive work

Trigger phrase:
"Daily Brief"

--------------------------------------------------
PROMPT
--------------------------------------------------

Run my Daily Command Brief.

Use:
- Today’s calendar
- Current sprint plan
- Active backlog
- Urgent inbox items (or ask me to paste them)

Return:
1) Critical path
   - Top 3 priorities today
   - Why each matters

2) Time audit
   - Calendar risks
   - Context switching
   - Missing prep blocks

3) Decision queue
   - Decisions I must make today
   - Ranked by impact

4) Risk flags
   - What is likely to slip or break

5) Follow-up radar
   - Threads or commitments at risk of going cold

6) Delegation list
   - What should move off my plate
   - Suggested owner (use Team Map)

Rules:
- Short sentences
- No fluff
- Under 400 words

==================================================
WORKFLOW 2: MEETING PREP BRIEF
==================================================

When to use:
- 10–15 minutes before any important meeting

Purpose:
- Enter meetings with intent
- Reduce rambling and misalignment
- Force decisions

Trigger phrase:
"Prep my next meeting"

--------------------------------------------------
PROMPT
--------------------------------------------------

Create a Meeting Prep Brief.

Use:
- Meeting title and attendees
- Relevant calendar context
- Recent email threads with attendees
- Related sprint items or backlog work

Return:
1) Why this meeting matters (1 sentence)
2) Decisions needed by the end (max 2)
3) My 5 best questions
4) Likely derailers or risks
5) One thing I must not leave without

Rules:
- Bullet format
- Direct
- Decision-focused

==================================================
WORKFLOW 3: POST-MEETING WRAP
==================================================

When to use:
- Immediately after a meeting

Purpose:
- Turn talk into execution
- Prevent dropped follow-ups
- Lock momentum while context is fresh

Trigger phrase:
"Wrap this meeting"

--------------------------------------------------
PROMPT
--------------------------------------------------

Post-Meeting Wrap.

Input:
- Meeting notes or transcript
- (If missing, ask me to paste notes)

Return:

A) Executive recap
   - 3–5 bullets

B) Decisions made
   Format:
   Decision | Why | Trade-offs | Next step

C) Action items
   Format:
   Task | Owner | Deadline | Priority | Dependency

D) Follow-ups mentioned but not assigned

E) Risks or unclear points (max 3)

Then:
- Draft the follow-up message in my voice
- Under 180 words
- Include owners and deadlines
- Propose next step and timing when appropriate

==================================================
WORKFLOW 4: INBOX TRIAGE
==================================================

When to use:
- Once or twice per day

Purpose:
- Turn inbox from stress into execution
- Surface what actually needs founder attention

Trigger phrase:
"Triage inbox"

--------------------------------------------------
PROMPT
--------------------------------------------------

Inbox Triage.

Review:
- New and unread messages
- Threads older than 48–72 hours
- Conversations involving customers, partners, or investors

Bucket into:
1) Must reply today
2) High value this week
3) Delegate
4) Archive

For "Must reply today":
- Draft replies in my voice
- Under 150 words
- Include next step, owner, and deadline when relevant

For "Delegate":
- Suggest best owner using Team Map
- Draft handoff message

Rules:
- Be decisive
- Reduce inbox volume aggressively

==================================================
WORKFLOW 5: DECISION MEMO
==================================================

When to use:
- When stuck
- When debating options
- When stakes are high

Purpose:
- Improve decision quality
- Reduce regret and rework

Trigger phrase:
"Decision memo"

--------------------------------------------------
PROMPT
--------------------------------------------------

I’m deciding between the following options:

Option A:
Option B:
(Optional: Option C)

Context:
Constraints:
What I’m optimizing for:

Do the following:
1) List assumptions I’m making (flag weak ones)
2) Steelman each option
3) Identify failure modes (3 / 6 / 12 months)
4) Reversibility test (easy vs expensive to undo)
5) Recommend one option

Then return:
- Decision
- Why
- Trade-offs
- Next 3 actions

Rules:
- No hedging
- Be direct

==================================================
WORKFLOW 6: WEEKLY CHIEF OF STAFF REVIEW
==================================================

When to use:
- End of week (Friday or Sunday)

Purpose:
- Close loops
- Reset priorities
- Prevent drift

Trigger phrase:
"Weekly review"

--------------------------------------------------
PROMPT
--------------------------------------------------

Weekly Chief of Staff Review.

Use:
- Sprint plan
- Backlog
- Calendar summary
- Open follow-ups

Return:
1) What moved (wins that matter)
2) What stalled (root cause)
3) What to cut (3 items)
4) What to delegate (item → owner)
5) Decisions I’m avoiding (ranked by impact)
6) Next week’s top 3 priorities
   - Include first action for each

Rules:
- Focus on execution, not reporting
- Short sentences
- No fluff
